1.3 Restore Factory Settings

If force restarting won’t help you to fix the problem you are facing, it’s time to reset your Nokia G22 to factory settings. The factory reset on an Android device will erase all of your phone settings, third-party apps, user data, and other app data to restore your device to its original manufacturer condition or settings. Step 1: Go to your ‘Settings” and then, go to “System”>” Advanced”>” Reset options”.

Step 2: Next, tap on “Erase all data”>” Reset phone” to factory reset on your phone. Here, you may need to enter the password or pin or pattern.
