## Part 1: How to turn off safe mode on Android?

**Method 1: Restarting the Realme 10T 5G device**

This is the easiest method to turn off the safe mode in Android. To use this method, follow the steps below.

Step 1 – Tap and hold the power button of your Realme 10T 5G.

Step 2 – You could find the option “Restart”. Tap on it. (If you have only one option, move to the step no 2)

Step 3 – Now, your phone will boot up in some time and you can see the Realme 10T 5G device is no more on safe mode.

The soft reset is very easy to perform. It won’t delete any of your personal files etc. Besides, it clears all the temp files and unnecessary data and recent apps so that you get a healthy device. This method is very good for turning off Safe mode on Android.

Step 1 – Tap and hold the power button.

Step 3 – Wait a few seconds and then turn it on again.

This time you can see your phone is no more in safe mode. Also, your junk files have been also removed. If you still find the Realme 10T 5G device is the safe mode, follow the next method.

**Method 3: Break all power**

This method is sometimes very helpful to turn off safe mode on android by disconnecting all the power as well as resetting the SIM card.

Step 4 – Turn the Realme 10T 5G device on by tapping and holding the power button.

Now, you can see your device is out of safe mode. If you still find your device in Safe mode, see the next method.

### **There are signs of soft bricking**

A touch screen not working on Realme 10T 5G smartphones indicates a soft brick (recoverable by firmware update), often caused by a defective or undercharged battery. System file corruption, old firmware, or failed upgrades are all possible causes. Several customers have complained that after installing a software update, their Realme 10T 5G 4 or 5 device's screen became unresponsive and remained at the boot logo.

### **There might be issues with the Realme 10T 5G device itself**

Screens on Realme 10T 5G devices may become unresponsive for a variety of hardware-related reasons. A broken LCD panel, faulty touch sensor, or loosened ribbon wires are all possible causes. Every Realme 10T 5G device, including the Note 4 and 5, is susceptible to these problems.

The Android operating system is adjustable. Some of our technological advancements may backfire on us.
