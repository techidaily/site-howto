Motorola G54 5G Screen Unresponsive? Here’s How to Fix It

Motorola G54 5G phones have become increasingly popular due to their advanced features, sleek designs, and affordable prices. What Causes A Motorola G54 5G Screen Unresponsive?

There are several reasons why a Motorola G54 5G phone’s screen may become unresponsive. Some common causes include software issues, hardware problems, or issues with the touchscreen itself. It’s essential to identify the root cause of the problem to fix it properly.

Below are some possible causes of Motorola G54 5G screen unresponsiveness, along with explanations of each.

  • Soft-bricked software: Soft-bricking refers to the state where the phone is stuck in an infinite boot loop or unable to boot. It can occur due to corrupt system files, outdated firmware, or unsuccessful updates. For example, some Motorola G54 5G Y2 and 5A users have reported their screens getting stuck on the boot logo after a software update, rendering the Motorola G54 5G device unusable.

  • Equipment is worn out: Hardware problems can also cause Motorola G54 5G screens to become unresponsive. It can be due to worn-out equipment, such as a damaged LCD screen, malfunctioning touch sensor, or loose ribbon cables. These issues can also occur in any Motorola G54 5G model, including the Y2 and 5A.

  • **Touchscreen problems:**Sometimes, the touchscreen itself can become unresponsive for various reasons. For instance, Motorola G54 5G Y2 and 5A users may face this issue if they have accidentally dropped their phone, exposed it to water, or used an incompatible screen protector. It can result in a damaged or malfunctioning touchscreen, making it difficult or impossible to operate the phone.

In the previous section, we identified the possible causes of Motorola G54 5G screen unresponsiveness, including soft-bricked software, hardware problems, and touchscreen issues. It is the most commonly used method to fix Motorola G54 5G’s touch screen and power button issues, such as unresponsiveness. By restarting the Motorola G54 5G device, background processes are disabled, and memory is freed up, allowing programs to run smoothly and potentially resolve the problem. To restart a Motorola G54 5G device, press the Power and Volume Up buttons simultaneously, and release the Power button once the Motorola G54 5Glogo appears.

Leaving the Motorola G54 5G device unused and draining the battery helps resolve these issues. After fully discharging the battery, the Motorola G54 5G device can be recharged and turned back on, potentially resolving the issue.

If restarting the Motorola G54 5G device or allowing the battery to drain doesn’t resolve the issue, seeking professional assistance is recommended. The manufacturer may provide additional troubleshooting steps or recommend sending the Motorola G54 5G device in for repair or replacement. Another solution is to back up the Motorola G54 5G device and perform a factory reset, which can help reset the Motorola G54 5G device to its original state and remove any software glitches that may be causing the screen unresponsiveness.
